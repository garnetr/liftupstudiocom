How To Use Bandit Signs To Sell Real Estate

In this article we will consider How To Use Bandit Signs To Sell Real Estate.

Put Bandit Signs in a few places around town. This special pole helps you staple your sign onto poles higher than other signs. - http://youtu.be/65iKo3XCiHY You can purchase from here: http://www.banditsignstapler.com They says you can also get them from home depot.

Another video talks about techniques about how not to get in trouble, and fined, by the sign police. Here is a series of videos on how to use them:
  * Part 1: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cXJbKhrN9RE
  * Part 2: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NXPZDw9tsr0
  * Part 3: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RhuoAJPG7Tc - Lots of advice about people who like to complain about signs.

#1 - Place your signs at night - you avoid traffic, and city employees.
#2 - At night the police have more important things to worry about.
#3 - Don't act suspicious, do it like you are doing what you are supposed to be doing, get the sign placed and then move on.
#4 - If anyone pushes back about the sign, then just move on to another location.
#5 - They can not fine you, until they find you. The city code enforcement are the ones to issue the fines.
#6 - Don't put your personal cell phone number on your sign, it makes it easy for the city to track you down. Get a free number thru google voice, or a number from different wifi calling apps such as talkatone. You can create a new google email, and a google voice number assigned to that google email. Also dont put the phone number of someone else on your signs, be sure to use a number that you control and can forward to anyone who can handle the calls for you.
#7 - Shopping centers are a great placement, and dont generate negative attention, except maybe from the security guards. But the property owners probably wont notice.
#8 - Nicer areas generate complaints quicker, and signs get taken down quicker.
#9 - Place signs on major traffic arteries
#10 - No confrontations! If someone complains to you while you out placing signs, pleasantly move on to another location. Maybe make a note about that location so you dont place a sign there in the future.
 
Dealing with the "sign police" - You may get phone call from someone from the city, or over eager clients, be very careful with them, often they are code enforcement people. Story at 3:50 about undercover copy with code enforcement who called to complain about a sign - https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RhuoAJPG7Tc

Let complainers who call you have their time to complain, be apologetic, and blaim it on your friend Pablo who didnt follow your directions.

Be considerate. Keep a list of where you have placed your signs, and remove them after your property has sold.
